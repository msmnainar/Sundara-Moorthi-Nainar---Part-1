# Business Memo

## Key Findings
Customers with fewer orders are more likely to churn
Users who are inactive carry a higher churn risk
Customers requiring more support tend to churn more often
Lower engagement levels are linked to higher churn rates
Campaigns contribute positively to customer retention

## Recommendations
Focus efforts on re-engaging inactive users
Enhance the quality of customer support
Avoid blanket discounts; use them selectively
Prioritize interventions for high-risk customer segments
